My Journey Throught coding ↓

  • March 2020 - The Beginning In my final exams, something surprising happened. I forgot my pencil, borrowed one, and noticed a classmate making mistakes. After the exams, there was chaos and rumors about extra holidays. But then, we found out about COVID-19. School closed, exams got canceled, and everyone moved to the next grade. At first, I was happy for the break, but watching the news made me realize it was a big deal. Things were about to change because of COVID-19.
  • April 2020 - August 2020 - Covid Becomes Powerfull When COVID-19 chaos hit, and a 21-day curfew was announced, things got really confusing. Stuck at home, I decided to make good use of the time. Inspired by my mom's Ph.D. work, I started learning to code using White Hat Junior. Later, I found out it was considered a scam, but back then, it cost a hefty 8000 rupees or about 100 US dollars for just eight classes or a week. during this entire time Covid-19 was booming in many countries
  • October 2020 - January 2021 - YouTube Comes for competetion In October, my focus was on White Hat Junior classes, realizing later that the expense was due to personalized 1-to-1 sessions. I discovered they used code.org for block-based coding and Firebase for script-based coding, a reward for attending over 100 classes. There was controversy, particularly a 12-year-old landing a Google job with a whopping ₹12,00,00,000 package after learning from them. Their ads, featuring Bollywood celebrities, aimed at selling courses to kids. I shifted to online tutorials, particularly Code with Harry, for HTML coding. Code.org offered an HTML-based website maker with free domains and CSS but no JavaScript support. I integrated lessons from Code with Harry into my previous projects. Then comings new year Covid gets another boom but scientist decide to make a vaccine and Youtube was also completing with Covid since it was Booming so fast 1 Billion hours worth of content where being watched
  • February 2021 - June 2021 - CodeItInYourWay Comes to Help During that time, online classes gained importance around June 2020. Many students, myself included, faced challenges such as poor internet connectivity, but I was fortunate to have broadband for the first six months. After that period, we acquired our own internet plans. Despite the challenges, I persevered in my online learning journey, and my coding skills flourished. A significant moment occurred when I participated in an Amfoss workshop by Amrita University, where I acquired knowledge in basics and IoT. It was a wise decision that not only enhanced my coding skills but also opened up new horizons in technology. However, for many others in India, the situation is not as positive. Due to the rapid spread of the new variant of Covid, people are succumbing quickly, and strict restrictions have been imposed.
  • June 2021 - December 2021 - CodeItInYourWay The better White Hat Jr? and WebOs:Hello is born The Amrita University workshop, lasting just five weeks, provided more tech insights than my five-month experience with Whitehat Junior. It sparked a realization: Bootstrap, a website framework introduced in the workshop, surpassed platforms like ProFreehost or Wix, which require payment. GitHub, a coding repository, became my go-to for industry professional-posted codes. In this competitive world, I envisioned creating something revolutionary – Web OS Hello, an operating system for the web. People in the workshop loved the idea, and it aligned with Microsoft's later launch of Windows 365 online. However, my project faced challenges, with limited awareness and visits, possibly due to my young age at the time, around 11 or 12 years old.But it sparked a light in me of making the best possible WebOS Version to complete with the Big Bulls of the Industry
  • January 2022 - April 2022 - Project "Godzilla" My biggest Failed Attempt After the Amrita University workshop, I had lifetime support, promising endless help. However, I didn't utilize it much, still following Code with Harry. I expanded my knowledge to Tailwind CSS, Vue.js, and Node.js. Then, I worked on Godzilla, aiming for the ultimate Web OS. This led to Project Jarvis, envisioning a unique design structure. A prototype on code.org impressed a friend, prompting an attempt at an app using Thunkable. Despite JavaScript knowledge, attempt 1 failed. Attempt 2 faced delays due to school and the pandemic. Modifying the failed design brought challenges, with GitHub Pages setup issues. The learning journey had its bumps, but the drive to create something exceptional persisted.Then i was soon Going to make Greatest WebOs which i ever made
  • April 2022 - June 2023 - Godzilla Become true So, what happened to Godzilla? Is it over? Well, not exactly. At one point, I considered leaving it behind to focus on studies. However, during the Christmas vacation, with time on my hands, I revived Godzilla, now known as Web OS Tripura. Why Tripura? It's a random name generated from all states and capitals, a cool coincidence. Development began, fueled by AI suggestions for the greatest website invention – a multi-tool site. Code with Harry tutorials helped bring Web OS Tripura to life, but it wasn't complete. The project stretched through January to March, and then a golden opportunity arose in April with a school workshop titled "How and Why?" Despite the workshop focusing on Wix, I wanted more. In just three weeks, I pulled off Web OS Tripura, showcasing what I'd learned.
  • May 2022 - Beginning of the New AI Web OS Tripura, or let's stick with Godzilla, was ready for action. However, Godzilla lacked many features compared to modern operating systems. My mentor suggested adding AI, and while the idea was enticing, creating a sophisticated chatbot like ChatGPT seemed impossible for a child. Instead, I opted for a brilliant solution—utilizing an old technology: the keyword system. By identifying keywords in user queries, I could craft responses. Yet, this approach had a flaw: it couldn't distinguish between similar queries. To market it effectively, I prepared a presentation script with pre-set questions. The AI wasn't super intelligent, but I cleverly incorporated a Google search bar to enhance its capabilities. People loved it, even though it couldn't compete with big players operating on a billion parameters, as I was working with less than 10. Godzilla continued, not just for AI but for the overall UI development.
  • June 2023 - Godzilla Wins his battle The presentation day arrived with anticipation. I had no idea about my opponents' projects, and they were equally clueless about mine—a strategic move. The competition featured various impressive projects, including games and an agriculture model using hydroponics. The judges evaluated projects, and I shared my past workshop experiences. The event concluded with our principal praising everyone's creativity. As the pandemic was ending, schools were set to reopen the following week, and I wondered if my classmates would recognize my efforts. After that week is over everything is done and the results came you won't believe it but Godzilla Actually won the 2022-23 HowNWhy workshop best Project Award but i didn't recive a award with a digital copy
  • July 2022 - December 2022 - A New Vision One of my biggest dreams for Web OS is to port it to mobile, creating a phone edition for public release. Unfortunately, it hasn't happened due to a lack of time and the setbacks in Godzilla's attempts. Delays, forgetfulness, and failed attempts were part of the journey. To promote myself, I decided to create this about page. While I may see myself as a brand, I remain humble. If you're curious about how I made the entire thing, you can contact me via email.
  • January 2023 - Present Presently i have no other ideas for coming up with a new product but rather updating the already existing one's if there is any i will let you guys know in the Updates tab
